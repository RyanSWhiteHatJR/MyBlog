Sydney Opera House

I visited the Opera House in 2019

The Sydney Opera House is a amazing and awesome building! The design looks super cool and inside is even cooler! They use the Opera House for opera, duh, my school has even contested against other schools in the Sydney Opera House for choir and band. It is an amazing place and it has an awesome view at the Harbour Bridge next to it, at the end of every year, the Opera House and the Harbour Bridge always launch fireworks into the sky at night time. In conclusion, the Opera House is an amazing building and I recommend you to visit it as well!

Sydney Sky Tower

I visited the Sky Tower in 2019

I visited the Sky Tower back in 2019, it's a very tall tower and it has a great view at the city, to access the Sky Tower, you have to buy a ticket at the bottom (Which is located at a shopping mall) then you ride a elevator all the way to the top where you get the best view at the entire city, very recently they have realsed a "Lego Exbitidion" there, I was one of the first people to enter and be shocked and amazed by all the lego sets and pieces. I would highley recommend you to visit the Sky Tower too!

World Wide News

  • LEGO reveals 21057 Singapore as latest Architecture Skyline set.

    Today LEGO has revealed that the latest set in the long-running Skyline series of Architecture sets will be 21057 Singapore. The 827-piece microscale model will feature some of the city-state’s most prominent landmarks, including the iconic Marina Bay Sands hotel, which also got its own standalone Architecture set back in 2013. Other locations in the skyline include the Lau Pa Sat market, One Raffles Place and OCBC skyscrapers, the posh Fullerton Hotel, the historic Boat Quay, and the distinctive and beautiful Gardens by the Bay nature park full of towering artificial trees covered in lush vegetation. The set will retail for US $59.99 | CAN $79.99 | UK £54.99 and will be available starting Jan. 1.

  • Amazon deforestation and number of fires show summer of 2019 not a "normal" year.

    The state of Rondônia in western Brazil — once home to 208,000 square kilometers of forest (about 51.4 million acres), an area slightly smaller than the state of Kansas — has become one of the most deforested parts of the Amazon. In the past three decades, clearing and degradation of the state’s forests have been rapid: 4,200 square kilometers cleared by 1978; 30,000 by 1988; and 53,300 by 1998. By 2003, an estimated 67,764 square kilometers of rainforest—an area larger than the state of West Virginia—had been cleared.

  • New Covid-19 variant found in South Africa.

    This variant has a large number of mutations, some of which are concerning. Preliminary evidence suggests an increased risk of reinfection with this variant, as compared to other VOCs. The number of cases of this variant appears to be increasing in almost all provinces in South Africa. Current SARS-CoV-2 PCR diagnostics continue to detect this variant. Several labs have indicated that for one widely used PCR test, one of the three target genes is not detected (called S gene dropout or S gene target failure) and this test can therefore be used as marker for this variant, pending sequencing confirmation. Using this approach, this variant has been detected at faster rates than previous surges in infection, suggesting that this variant may have a growth advantage.
